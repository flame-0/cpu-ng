The Computer Professionals Union adds its voice to the growing discontent of the Filipino people and its ICT professionals over the lack of accountability in the Marcos Jr. administration. The CPU was founded from the collective call of ICT professionals for accountability during the EDSA II movement 25 years ago, and now we hold true to our roots and once again echo CPU's calls for a comprehensive solution for the systemic problems that our country faces.

The tech community has seen firsthand how this culture of impunity paralyzes national progress. During Sara Duterte’s tenure as Secretary of the Department of Education (DepEd), the Commission on Audit (COA) flagged severe delays and abysmal completion rates in the agency's 2023 infrastructure programs. While public school teachers and students starved for genuine technological integration, they were met with administrative bottlenecks and substandard, low-end hardware. This follows the Rodrigo Duterte's administration's own legacy of corruption — VP Duterte's predecessor in DepEd was marred by the ₱2.4-billion procurement of overpriced, obsolete Intel Celeron laptops under former Secretary Leonor Briones.

Accountability doesn't stop at VP Duterte, and must be extended to include the Chief Executive. President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. must be held responsible not only for giving VP Duterte the platform they now hold, but for his own office's controversies: their own confidential funds ballooning even worse than VP Duterte's, and their direct involvement as high overseer and director to the whole government's flood control corruption controversy. He selectively targets who gets to be held accountable and pretends to be our champion against corruption, all the while hiding the biggest acts of corruption committed under his authority and supervision. We must act now before the state of science and technology in the country further suffers because of corruption on the highest levels of our government. Lahat ng sangkot, dapat managot!
